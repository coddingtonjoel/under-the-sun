BODY

Descartes believes that nothing is ever truly perceived by bodily senses because the perception and interpretation comes through the mind.

Bacon believes the greatest hindrance to human understanding is the senses and that senses themselves are prone to error.

Pascal believes that the priniciples of truth are reason and senses. Both of them disagree with each other, causing us to experience natural error.

If our senses are prone to error, how as thinking beings can we discern wisdom?

We can't without God.

BUT WHAT IS WISDOM?

“But where shall wisdom be found? … Man does not know its worth, and it is not found in the land of the living” (Job 28:12-13)

"For the Lord gives wisdom; from his mouth come knowledge and understanding" (Proverbs 2:6)

Proverbs explains that the fear of the Lord is foundational for obtaining wisdom. By fearing the Lord, we allow his absolute sovereignty to guide our choices made by our error-prone human nature.
